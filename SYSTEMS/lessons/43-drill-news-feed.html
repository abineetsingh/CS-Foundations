Drill: News Feed

The fan-out question, finally answered in full: push vs pull, the celebrity problem, and the hybrid every real feed runs. Timer on.

Why this matters

"Design the Twitter/Instagram feed" is the genre's crown jewel because one decision — when is the feed assembled? — cascades through storage, caching, and cost. You've circled it since lesson 11; today you own it. This is also where your two prior drills compound: run the 40 stages briskly and spend everything on stage 5.

The prompt

Home timeline for 200 M DAU: users follow others; the feed shows followees' recent posts, newest-first, infinite scroll. Median user follows 400, is followed by 400. A celebrity has 100 M followers. Feed load p99 < 300 ms.

Stages 1–4, briskly (20 min)

Compare your answer

Envelope verdicts: reads (feed loads, ~10/user/day = 20k QPS avg, 100k peak) dwarf writes (~2 posts/user/day ≈ 5k/s); the real multiplier hides in fan-out: one median post touches 400 followers' feeds. API: GET /feed?cursor=… (cursor, not offset — 02), POST /posts. Data: posts (12's schema + (user_id, created_at) index — 13), follows, and — the new table — a feed cache per user (Redis list of post_ids, capped ~800 entries). HLD: 11's diagram + a queue (23) between posting and feed-building. Ranking: descope to reverse-chron aloud ("ranking is an ML system on top; the infrastructure question is the same").

Stage 5 — the fan-out fork (25 min)

Attempt each before opening: (a) define fan-out-on-write vs fan-out-on-read and cost each with the envelope numbers. (b) now the celebrity posts — what breaks in each model? (c) design the hybrid.

Compare: (a) the two pure models

Fan-out-on-write (push): when Ada posts, a worker fleet (23) prepends the post_id into each of her 400 followers' Redis feed lists. Feed read = one Redis fetch: O(1), ~1 ms — the p99 budget loves it. Cost: 5k posts/s × 400 = 2M list-writes/s (workers + Redis scale for this — feasible), and work is wasted on dormant users (skip fan-out to users inactive >30 days; they rebuild on return).

Fan-out-on-read (pull): feed load = fetch your 400 followees' recent posts (400 index hits — 13's (user_id, created_at) earning its keep) and merge — your DSA k-way merge (heap!) in production. No write amplification, always fresh; but 400 reads + merge × 100k QPS at p99<300 ms = the read path melts. Pure pull fails the latency requirement; SAY that with the numbers.

Compare: (b) the celebrity breaks push

One post → 100 M list-writes. At even 1M/s that's 100 seconds of a saturated worker fleet — per celebrity post; a busy news day melts feed freshness for everyone (hot-key, 16, in its most famous costume). Pull, meanwhile, handles celebrities fine: their posts are read straight from the posts table (hot → cached hard, 09 — one key serving millions of readers is exactly what caches are for).

Compare: (c) the hybrid — the real answer

Push for the many, pull for the few. Users below a follower threshold (~100k) fan out on write; celebrities don't. Feed read = your precomputed Redis list + merge-at-read of posts from the handful of celebrities you follow (bounded: nobody follows a million celebrities — the merge is ~5-way, milliseconds). Each side's weakness is covered by the other's strength — every large feed you use runs a variant of this. Close with ops instincts: feed-list TTLs + rebuild-from-pull as the fallback (a lost Redis node = slow feeds, not lost data — 36 degradation, since posts remain the source of truth and feeds are derived data — 20's pattern).
