Cloud Building Blocks

One skill, learned today: translate every concept you've built into the AWS/GCP names it wears in production — plus the two genuinely new ideas the cloud adds: regions and serverless.

Why this matters

Job postings, interviews, and engineering blogs speak vendor: "we moved it to SQS," "an EC2 fleet behind an ALB." The concepts are all yours already — this lesson is a phrasebook, not new grammar. Concepts over vendor trivia, per your mission's own out-of-scope list.

The deeper pattern: managed services sell you the ops, not the concept. RDS is Postgres where failover (15/26), backups, and patching are someone else's pager. You trade money and some control for operational burden — usually the right trade until you're enormous. In interviews, either layer is acceptable; naming both ("a queue — SQS, say") reads as experience.

The two genuinely new ideas

1. Regions & availability zones. A region is a geographic cluster of datacenters; an availability zone (AZ) is one independent datacenter within it (own power, cooling, network). The design defaults that follow from your own latency table (05): spread replicas across AZs for free-ish fault tolerance (~1–2 ms apart — a whole-datacenter failure becomes a non-event); go multi-region only for geographic latency or disaster recovery, because 50–150 ms between regions makes synchronous replication painful — multi-region is where all of phase 3's hard tradeoffs (18's PACELC, multi-leader conflicts) come home to roost.

2. Serverless. Functions-as-a-service (Lambda / Cloud Functions): upload a handler; the platform runs an instance per request, scaling zero-to-thousands automatically, billed per invocation. The catches you should recite: cold starts (a fresh instance pays init latency — the p99 tax, 35), statelessness is mandatory (07 enforced by the platform), and per-invocation pricing crosses over managed fleets at sustained high volume. Sweet spot: spiky, event-driven glue — queue consumers (23), webhooks, cron. Your API fleet at steady 10k QPS: containers. [AWS: Lambda concepts]

AI-lab tie-in

AI-lab job postings are written in this phrasebook — "S3-based data lake," "SQS-fed inference queues," "EKS with GPU node pools." And the GPU cloud adds one vendor-y wrinkle worth knowing: capacity reservations. GPUs are scarce enough that "just autoscale" fails; labs reserve fleets years ahead — a supply-chain constraint your envelope math (06) never had to price in before.
